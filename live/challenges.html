IBM - Research Challenge

More Info

Drug Repurposing for Cancer - A Data Driven Approach

IBM Research in collaboration with Cures Within Reach for Cancer is on an ambitious initiative to automatically analyze and mine the plethora of scientific publications and real-world data on non-cancer generic drugs to identify the most promising therapies to repurpose for cancer treatment.In this year's IBM TechTegether Challenge, we ask you to join us to tackle this grand challenge together. As an innovative developer and out-of-the-box thinker, you are asked to develop a scalable and easy-to-use automation tool in the quest of analyzing scientific publications and data on generic drugs for cancer treatments.

You will learn what type of data needs to be collected on drugs and cancers from scientific paper abstracts to make it possible to automatically extract useful and insightful information, and how your tools developed through this IBM Techtogether Challenge will help us to achieve such a goal with more accurate labeled data. You will even get a chance to use the tools developed by other teams (or, equivalently, let other teams use your tools) to see how your efforts can make a difference in collecting useful data, and finally programmatically measure the quality of the data you collect. For those who would like to take on an even bigger challenge, we have something extra for you to flex your machine learning and AI brain muscles.

Prize: 2020 Summer Internship with IBM Research.*

*Note: if you are not eligible for an internship with IBM Research, (meaning that your education will be completed in the Spring of 2020), we will instead offer you a chance to interview for a full-time position.

Best Environmental Sustainability Hack

More Info
  1. Create a solution for your neighborhood that reduces a waste of your choice

    2. OR

  2. Create a platform/avenue that actively engages building users and the general public with the BERDO data to spur sustainable action (energy efficiency, retrofits, and upgrades)

Background: Buildings account for over seventy percent of greenhouse gas emissions in Boston. The Building Energy Reporting and Disclosure Ordinance (BERDO) makes building owners, tenants, and other stakeholders more aware of their energy usage and greenhouse gas emissions and opportunities to reduce both. It also requires the City to make this data public. Buildings covered must also show concerted efforts to reduce their emissions every five years through energy actions or audits. We want to make this data accessible to the general public and hold these building owners accountable. Green benchmarks or scorecards have been explored in other cities, and we want to start the culture in Boston.

  2. Analyze Boston
  3. Central Library Electricity Usage
  4. City Hall Electricity Usage
  5. COB Utility Data
  6. Greenhouse Gas Emissions
  7. Rainfall Fata
  8. A tone of climate ready maps
